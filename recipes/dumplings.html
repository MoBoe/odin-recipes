Dumplings

What Are Dumplings?

A dumpling is a small mass of dough that can be boiled, fried, baked, or steamed. There are a ton of dumpling varieties out there, from pork- and cabbage-filled Chinese dumplings to cheesy Polish dumplings. boiled drop dumplings, which are popular in the U.S., are often paired with chicken and broth to create a popular comfort food called chicken and dumplings.

They're prepared by dropping spoonfuls of dough into a pot of boiling soup or stew. The starch in the dumplings thickens the broth, resulting in a rich stew that's impossible to resist.

Dumpling Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Baking Powder
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Butter
  • Milk

Steps

  1. Stir together flour, baking powder,sugar, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Cut in butter until mixture is crumbly.
  3. Stir in milk and mix until a batter forms that is thick enough to be scooped with a spoon.
  4. Allow batter to rest for 3 to 5 minutes..
  5. Drop batter by spoonfuls into boiling stew or soup.
  6. Cover and simmer without lifting the lid for 15 minutes. Serve.
